# Rewrite Frameworks

## Past → Present → Future (default, for "why are you looking" / "why us")

Three parts, in order, each one sentence or two — no more:

1. **Past (factual, neutral)** — why you're leaving/left, stated as fact
   with zero negative charge. No "сократили", no complaints. If there's
   nothing negative to neutralize, this can just be "моё сотрудничество
   с X завершилось" or "проект закрылся".
2. **Present (motivation)** — what you're good at and genuinely interested
   in doing next. This is about *you*, stated with confidence, not about
   what you're escaping.
3. **Future (value to them)** — how that interest/skill maps onto a problem
   this specific company likely has. Needs to be specific enough that it
   couldn't be copy-pasted into a different company's application
   unchanged — generic "уверен, что буду полезен" doesn't count.

Example shape (not a template to fill blindly — adapt wording to the user's
actual situation):

> Моё сотрудничество с [компанией/проектом] завершилось [neutral reason].
> За это время я сфокусировался на [specific skill/domain], и сейчас хочу
> применить этот опыт там, где [specific problem]. По описанию роли у вас
> это выглядит именно так, что и привлекло меня откликнуться.

## STAR (for behavioral/conflict questions)

Use when the question is specifically about a situation, not a general
motivation question — "расскажите о конфликте", "как вы справлялись с
сложной ситуацией", "пример провала".

1. **Situation** — brief, neutral context. No blame language.
2. **Task** — what was expected of you specifically.
3. **Action** — what *you* did. This is the longest part — concrete,
   first-person, no vague "мы решили".
4. **Result** — outcome, ideally with a number or a verifiable change. If
   the result was a failure, name what you learned and changed afterward
   — that's often a stronger answer than a clean success story.

Keep other people in the story neutral — describe their role/actions
factually, not their character ("руководитель не давал обратную связь" is
fine; "руководитель был токсичным" is not).

## Tone calibration by company type

- **Российская корпорация / госкомпания** — conservative, avoid anything
  that reads as politically loaded; loyalty and stability framing lands
  better here than "drive change" language; keep ambition framed as
  contribution, not disruption.
- **Стартап** — more room for ambition and ownership language ("хочу брать
  на себя больше"), but still avoid the manifesto pattern (#3 in
  RED-FLAGS.md) — startups want doers, not visionaries who haven't shipped
  yet.
- **Международная компания / международная команда** — process/structure
  language and direct, concise answers land well; RF-specific sensitivities
  (sanctions, work restrictions) can be stated as plain logistics rather
  than something to euphemize.
- **Не уверен(а) / не указано** — default to the conservative calibration
  and flag to the user that the answer may need adjusting once the target
  is known.
